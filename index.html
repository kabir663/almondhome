Tree Profile

Name of Tree

  • Common Name: The Indian Almond Tree
  • Scientific Name: Terminalia catappa

The Indian Almond Tree (Terminalia catappa) is a resilient and fast-growing tree, thriving in coastal and tropical regions. Its leaves, which are large, oval, and glossy, provide essential shade for the environment and human habitation. The tree's bark is rough and brown, often used in traditional crafting. During the monsoon season, the Indian Almond Tree produces small white or greenish-yellow flowers that are highly aromatic, attracting a range of pollinators, including bees and butterflies..

Its fruit, which resembles a green or yellow almond, is not only edible but also used in various cultural rituals and as a source of food for wildlife.
